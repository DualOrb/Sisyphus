# Tablet Setup Guide

Reference for helping restaurants connect and set up their Valley Eats tablets. Covers both flat tablets and iLeap tablets.

## Tablet Versions

There are four tablet models in circulation. Always check the tablet version before helping a restaurant. The model is found on the restaurant page: click restaurant -> restaurant pages -> change details -> scroll down to tablet model.

| Model | Description |
|---|---|
| Telpo TPS570 | Older tablet, orange with printer |
| HAOQIN H7_US | Older flat tablet, without printer |
| SUNMI V2 | Flat tablet |
| iLeap (alps POSH5-OS01) | Newer orange tablet with printer |

## Initial Connection and Set-Up (Flat Tablets: HAOQIN, SUNMI)

Restaurants are instructed to call the support team when having trouble setting up a new tablet.

### Step 1: Connect to Wi-Fi

1. Navigate to the home screen or pull down the control panel.
2. Locate the settings icon or Wi-Fi shortcut.
3. Find the restaurant's Wi-Fi network and connect.

### Step 2: Volume and Time Zone

1. Set the volume to maximum.
2. Go to System -> Date & Time settings.
3. Enable "Use network-provided time."
4. Verify the time zone matches the restaurant's local time zone.
5. Confirm the displayed time is correct.

### Step 3: Open App and Enter ID

1. Launch the Valley Eats POS App from the home screen.
2. Enter the Restaurant ID and log in. The ID is the first 8 alphanumeric characters after "/restaurant/" in the app URL, found on the restaurant's page.

### Step 4: Test Order and Notifications

Perform this with a Valley Eats team member:

1. Set the restaurant's hours to open.
2. Send a test order.
3. Verify the order appears on screen with a sound notification.
4. Decline the order with reason: "test."
5. Set the restaurant's hours back to closed until they are ready to launch.

Note: The majority of new tablet setup is handled by the Restaurant Growth Team. Support staff need this knowledge for when newer partners have tablet troubles.

## Troubleshooting: Tablet Won't Turn On

1. Ask the restaurant if the tablet is plugged in.
2. If yes and it still won't turn on, ask them to try a different outlet.
3. If it still won't turn on, the tablet likely needs replacement. Ask if they want to be halted or if they want us to call in their orders. Create a ticket for Growth to replace the tablet.

## Troubleshooting: Orders Not Showing

1. Ask the restaurant if their internet is connected.
2. If they say yes, send them a test message in the restaurant portal. Tell them not to touch the tablet while you send the message.
3. If the message comes through: the tablet is working and internet is connected.
4. If the message does not come through: there is no endpoint. Proceed to the cache/data clear process below.

## Troubleshooting: Network/Internet Issues

1. Check if other devices at the restaurant are connected to the internet.
   - If no other devices connect either, their internet may be down. Proceed to step 2.
   - If other devices connect fine, skip to step 3.
2. Restart the modem/router.
3. Turn Wi-Fi off and back on on the tablet.
4. Disconnect from the network, forget the network, then reconnect.
5. Restart the tablet.

## Troubleshooting: Date and Time Incorrect

If you notice a restaurant's confirmation timestamps are inaccurate:

1. Navigate to Settings.
2. Open the Date & Time section.
3. Make sure "Set to network's time zone" is enabled.
4. Reconfirm the date and time are correct.

## Resetting Flat Tablet and iLeap Tablet (Cache/Data Clear)

This fixes the "no endpoint" problem on HAOQIN H7_US and SUNMI V2 tablets:

1. Go to Settings.
2. Go to Apps.
3. Select the Valley Eats App.
4. Go to Storage.
5. Clear Cache.
6. Clear Data.
7. Delete app data (press OK).
8. Press the middle circle (home button) at the bottom of the tablet.
9. Open the Valley Eats app and log back in.

After this process, the restaurant will need the App URL to log back in. Use only the first 8 characters after "restaurant/" from the restaurant page.

Note for HAOQIN tablets: The app will not show right away after reset. It will display approximately 25 apps. The restaurant must scroll down to find the Valley Eats app, then continue with the login process.

## Resetting Old Tablet (Telpo TPS570 - Orange with Printer)

1. Tap "Telpo" 6 times.
2. Tap "Operator" 6 times.
3. Tap "Admin."
4. Enter password: 654321 and tap "Log In."
5. Select "Enter Android."
6. Tap the three dots menu -> Settings -> Apps -> Valley Eats App -> Storage -> Clear Cache -> Clear Data.

## Hard Reset (Telpo TPS570)

Hold the power button. If the tablet does not turn on, the restaurant may need a new tablet. Create a ticket for the restaurant team.
