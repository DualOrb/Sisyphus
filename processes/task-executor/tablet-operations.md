# Tablet Operations Guide

Day-to-day tablet operations for restaurant partners: halt, ETA, messenger, orders, and receiving orders.

## Halt

The Halt function stops new orders from being placed at the restaurant. When a restaurant presses the Halt icon on their tablet, their restaurant goes offline and customers cannot place orders.

### When restaurants use Halt

- The kitchen is backed up with orders.
- Equipment malfunctions, power outages, or similar issues.
- Closing earlier than their normal close time.

### Key rule about Halt

Halting a restaurant resets the following morning. The next day the restaurant will resume on its regular schedule automatically. The halt does not carry over.

### How to halt a restaurant from dispatch

Navigate to the restaurant's page and change their status to "Halted." Inform the restaurant that they can also do this themselves on the tablet by pressing the Halt button.

## ETA

The ETA feature allows restaurants to adjust the estimated time it takes for food to be ready. This affects:

- The time the courier will show up for pickup.
- The time estimate shown to the customer for how long the order will take to prepare.

Only adjust the ETA when absolutely necessary.

## Messenger

Messenger is the communication gateway between restaurant partners and the Valley Eats support team. This is where we notify the restaurant when:

- A driver is delayed for pickup.
- An order needs to be delayed or sped up.
- An order needs to be confirmed.

Important: Always call restaurants directly if an issue arises, because they may not always be checking the tablet messenger.

## Profile

Through the Profile icon on the tablet, a restaurant can:

- Make changes to their operating hours.
- Access the web portal link to make changes to their menu.

Reminder: Always hit the Publish button to save any changes made through the profile.

## Orders/History

The Orders/History section shows the restaurant's order history for the past 10 days. Use this to reference back to a previous order for any reason.

## Receiving Orders

### When an order is received

1. Confirm the order using the confirm button.
2. Verify the order ready time. Preorders will show up in blue.
3. Verify the quantities of ordered items.
4. Call our support team if the order requires a substitution or a time adjustment.
5. Adjust the ETA if there are multiple orders and extra preparation time is needed for future orders.

### Packaging the order

1. Place all ordered items in the takeout bag.
2. Label each bag with the corresponding Order ID.
3. If there are multiple bags for one order, label them sequentially (1 of 3, 2 of 3, 3 of 3) along with the Order ID.
4. Attach the order receipt to the takeout bag.
5. If the order is a pickup order, mark the order as "ready" on the tablet to notify the customer that they can pick up the order.
6. When the pickup order is collected by the customer, mark it as completed so it is removed from the active orders screen.

### Courier pickup

1. Confirm the Order ID and contents with the driver upon arrival.
2. Place or allow the driver to set the delivery order in the thermal bag.

## Unconfirmed Orders

If an order goes unconfirmed, contact the restaurant. If they are not responding, check if the tablet is online. If the tablet appears offline, follow the troubleshooting steps in the tablet-setup-guide.

## Orders Don't Show on Tablet

1. Press the refresh icon in the top right corner of the app.
2. Power cycle the tablet (hold the power button to restart).
