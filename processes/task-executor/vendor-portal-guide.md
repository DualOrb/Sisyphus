# Vendor Portal Guide

How the Vendor Portal works, and what restaurants and dispatch can do through it.

## Accessing the Vendor Portal

1. Go to https://vendors.valleyeats.ca/.
2. Log in with the same credentials used for dispatch.
3. You will see a page listing all restaurants, organized alphabetically by market (e.g., Arnprior, Deepriver, Embrun, etc.).
4. Find the restaurant by scrolling to its market or using the "Search Restaurant" tab in the upper right corner.

## Navigating to a Restaurant

1. Click on the restaurant you want to access.
2. You will be taken to their Profile & Settings page.
3. On the left side, under the restaurant's logo, click "Menu" to access menu items.

## Disabling/Pausing a Menu Item

There are two ways to find and pause an item:

### Method 1: Through Menu Sections

1. Click "Menu" on the left side of the restaurant's profile page.
2. Browse to the section where the item is listed (e.g., "Hashbrown" would be under the breakfast section).
3. Click the pause button underneath the item.
4. The item should now appear paused/grayed out.

### Method 2: Through Search

1. On the menu page, use the search bar in the upper right corner.
2. Find the item in the results.
3. Click the "Available" button to disable it. It will turn gray.
4. Click "Publish" at the top of the page to save the change and disable the item on the live menu.

### Disabling Toppings, Sides, or Drinks in Combos

Toppings (e.g., ketchup, pickles, green olives) cannot be found through normal search or in menu sections. You also need to do this when pausing a drink or side so it is paused in combos as well.

1. Select your restaurant in the Vendor Portal.
2. Click "Modifiers" at the top of the screen.
3. Search for the item you need to disable.
4. Click on each section that appears in the search results. Example: searching "apple juice" might show "Choice of Drink."
5. Within each section, click the pause button beside the item.
6. Important: You may have multiple sections appear. You must click on EACH one and pause the item in EVERY section to ensure it is fully paused across all combos and menus.

## Adjusting Restaurant Hours

### One-Time Change

If the restaurant's hours change is for one day only:

1. In the Vendor Portal, select the market and restaurant.
2. Click "Hours" at the top left.
3. Click "Holiday Hours."
4. Create a Holiday Hours entry for that day with the adjusted times.
5. Holiday Hours will automatically revert to regular hours once the day has passed.

If it is close enough to closing time, sometimes a halt is easier so less adjustment work needs to be done. Confirm with the restaurant if that approach is acceptable.

### Permanent Change

1. Navigate to the Vendor Portal, select the market and restaurant.
2. Click "Hours" at the top left.
3. Select the new time from the dropdown menu.
4. Click "Update Hours."
5. Refresh the page to confirm the change has been applied.
6. Confirm with the restaurant if any orders need to be picked up earlier.
7. Make a note of the change for the restaurant relations coordinator, including the new hours and why they were changed.

### Critical Rules for Hours Changes

- **Always verify that the open time is before the close time and that AM/PM is set correctly.**
- **Split hours (e.g., open for breakfast, closed for lunch, open for dinner): DO NOT TOUCH THE HOURS.** Split hours must be manually edited by the technical team. Editing them directly will break the split hours in the system. Make a note/ticket and pass it to a supervisor. Halt the restaurant if needed in the meantime.

## Halting a Restaurant

1. Navigate to the restaurant's page in the Vendor Portal.
2. Change their status to "Halted."
3. Inform the restaurant that they can also halt themselves on the tablet by pressing the Halt button.
4. Halting resets the following morning. The restaurant will resume its regular schedule automatically the next day.

## Sending a Test Message

To test whether a restaurant's tablet is connected:

1. Open the restaurant portal.
2. Send a test message (tell the restaurant not to touch the tablet while you send it).
3. If the message comes through: the tablet is working and the internet is connected.
4. If the message does not come through: there is no endpoint. Follow the cache/data clear reset process in the tablet-setup-guide.

## What Restaurants Can Do Through the Vendor Portal

Restaurants with portal access can:

- View and manage their menu items.
- Adjust their operating hours.
- View their profile and settings.

## What Dispatch Can Do Through the Vendor Portal

- Pause/disable menu items (never delete).
- Re-enable previously paused items.
- Adjust restaurant hours (one-time via Holiday Hours or permanent).
- Disable toppings and modifier items.
- View restaurant profile and settings.
- Send test messages to verify tablet connectivity.
- Halt restaurants.
- Access the menu portal for creating and editing items, options, and modifiers.
