# Scenario: Courier Going Long Distance in Multiple Directions

**Source:** Dispatch Analyst Guide -- Scenarios section

---

## When This Happens

When courier resources are limited, the likelihood of a courier delivering multiple orders in opposite directions increases. This leads to an increase in potential order delays. This is especially common with cross-market deliveries (e.g., Pembroke to Petawawa, Carleton Place to Almonte) which pull couriers out of their home market.

---

## Procedure

### Step 1: Modify Order Ready Times Immediately

- As soon as you identify a courier heading long distance with conflicting directions, adjust order ready times to reduce potential delays.
- Only modify ready times if you have an accurate update from the restaurant and/or courier.
- If the change is greater than 10 minutes, call the restaurant with as much notice as possible -- the restaurant may have already started preparing the order for the original time.
- When possible, adjust ready times earlier rather than delaying them. Call the restaurant to confirm they can prepare for the adjusted time.

### Step 2: Ensure Courier Delivers Closest Orders First

- Review the delivery addresses on the map by clicking the address under the "Customer" column to see where each delivery goes.
- Instruct the courier to deliver from closest to furthest to ensure maximum food freshness and minimize backtracking.
- Use the "Ready" and "Delivery" times to predict where the courier will be and when, and leave breathing room for unexpected issues.

### Step 3: Adjust Market Delay

- Increase the market delay to ensure no new orders conflict with the courier's long distance trip.
- The market delay controls how much time is given for a courier to arrive at a restaurant after an order is confirmed.
- This prevents the system from assigning additional orders that the courier cannot realistically reach.

---

## Additional Considerations

### Cross-Market Deliveries

- Orders with red and blue backgrounds on the dispatch view indicate cross-market deliveries.
- Bundle cross-market orders as much as possible to avoid losing too many couriers from their home market.
- Try to plan a return trip for the courier (e.g., if they deliver Pembroke to Petawawa, try to give them a Petawawa to Pembroke order or local Petawawa orders to make the drive back worthwhile).

### Courier Pay Top-Ups

- Long-distance cross-market orders may qualify for a top-up (typically $4.00 + $0.50 gas = $4.50).
- Top-ups should not be added to an order until it is completed/delivered.
- Rules vary by market -- check the specific top-up guidelines for the route.

### Courier Communication

- If a courier tells you they do not want to do long-distance orders, respond professionally: acknowledge their concern, explain that part of the courier position is to handle all orders that come in, and direct further questions to relations@valleyeats.ca.
- Couriers are independent contractors. You cannot force them, but you can explain the expectation.

### Rerouting If Needed

- If the long-distance route is truly unworkable, reroute one or more orders to a different courier.
- To reroute: click the courier's 2-character moniker on the dispatch view, select another active courier, "anyone else" (auto-assigns avoiding the current driver), or "surprise me" (system picks best option).
- An order can be rerouted at any point before the courier is marked "At Restaurant."
- If the courier is already en route, call them to let them know about the change so they can redirect immediately.

### Avoid Overloading

- Do not give any single courier more than three (3) in-bag orders at one time, as this may affect food quality.
- Use the courier utilization heads-up display: Red = overloaded, Yellow = conflicting orders, Green = OK, Blue = empty.

---

## Escalation

- If the delivery radius needs to be reduced due to insufficient courier resources, notify a supervisor -- only supervisors can adjust the delivery radius.
- If you are past your maximum delay despite adjustments, a supervisor may decide to move to "Takeout Only" to focus on clearing current orders.
