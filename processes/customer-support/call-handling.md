# Call Handling Procedures

**Trigger:** Inbound or outbound phone call via call center.

**Check First:**
- Call center status is set correctly (Available, Outbound, etc.)
- Customer's order number (ask immediately)

**Steps:**
1. **Answer with:** "Thank you for calling Valley Eats, this is [name] speaking. How can I help you today?"
2. Apologize for any issues, then ask for order number immediately -- you cannot help without it.
3. **Statuses:** Offline = no incoming calls. Available = receiving calls (auto 1:30 delay between). Outbound = outgoing only. ACW = post-call work. Lunch/Personal = breaks.
4. **Transferring:** tell customer you are transferring, hit Quick Connects, brief the receiving person, hit Join (3-way), then Leave Call.
5. **Outbound:** some customers screen unknown numbers; may need 2 attempts for urgent situations.
6. **Late order call:** pull up order, check timestamps (ready, at-restaurant, picked-up, delivery), communicate status honestly.
7. **Missing items call:** confirm order number on bag, determine fault (restaurant vs courier).
8. **Customer wants supervisor:** do not argue. Create/assign ticket with all info, tell customer supervisor will reach out.
9. **Restaurant call:** help directly if possible (confirm orders, disable items, troubleshoot). Otherwise create ticket.
10. **Courier shift issues:** Mon-Fri 8am-4pm direct to Driver Relations. Outside hours, handle yourself.
11. **End of shift:** set status to Offline, gear icon > Log out, close window.
12. **Never give staff phone numbers** to customers or restaurants without permission.

**Escalate If:**
- Customer demands to speak with supervisor
- Fraud or stolen card situation
- Issue you cannot resolve after troubleshooting
