# Call Center and Call Handling Procedures

How to handle inbound and outbound calls in the Valley Eats call center.

---

## Call Center Overview

The call center is where all phone communication with customers, restaurants, and couriers happens. It automatically records all calls for quality assurance purposes. The customer hears a message letting them know the call will be recorded.

---

## Logging In

1. Click the chevron on the support panel to expand the call center.
2. From the Contacts tab, click the "Launch Call Center" button.
3. Log in with your credentials.
4. Select one of the available statuses.

---

## Call Center Statuses

Set your status using the top dropdown menu near the cloud icon.

| Status | Meaning |
|--------|---------|
| **Offline** | Default status. You receive no incoming calls but can still make outbound calls. |
| **Available** | You receive incoming calls and can make outbound calls. After a call, there is an automatic 1:30 minute delay before another call comes through, giving you time between calls. |
| **Outbound** | You can only make outbound calls. No incoming calls. |
| **ACW** (After Call Work) | Use when you need extra time after a call to finish up related work. No incoming calls. |
| **Lunch** | Use when taking a break. No incoming calls. |
| **Personal** | Use for personal needs like a washroom break. No incoming calls. |

---

## Answering Inbound Calls

Always answer with this greeting:

> "Thank you for calling Valley Eats, this is [your name] speaking. How can I help you today?"

### First Steps After Greeting

1. Apologize for any issues the customer has been experiencing.
2. Ask for the customer's order number. This is the fastest approach — you cannot do anything about the issue until you have the order in front of you and can see what is wrong.

---

## Making Outbound Calls

1. Type a phone number or paste one in (Ctrl+V).
2. Press "Call."

Note: Some customers may think the number is a spam call. If the situation is urgent, it may require more than one attempt.

---

## Transferring Calls

Follow these steps in order:

1. While talking to the customer, let them know you are going to transfer them.
2. Hit "Quick Connects" or enter a new number, and select "Call." The customer is automatically placed on hold so you can speak privately.
3. Give the person you are transferring to a rundown of the situation first.
4. Hit the "Join" button. All three of you can now talk.
5. Exit the call by selecting the "Leave Call" button.

---

## Accessing Staff Contacts

The call center contains all staff members' phone numbers in the "Contacts" tab, in case you need to reach someone urgently about an issue.

**Important:** Never give a staff member's number to a customer or restaurant without that staff member's permission.

---

## End of Shift

1. Change your status to "Offline."
2. Click the gear icon.
3. Select "Log out."
4. Close the call center window.

---

## Call Handling Best Practices

- **First impressions matter.** Be positive, genuine, use the customer's name, and solve problems quickly.
- **Active listening.** Understand the problem correctly and anticipate follow-up questions.
- **Be confident.** Confidence builds trust and professionalism.
- **Be decisive.** Tell the customer clearly what we can do for them. Hesitation reduces satisfaction.
- **Be empathetic.** Show that you understand how the customer is feeling. Emotional connections build lasting relationships.
- **Document while you wait.** While the phone is ringing or the customer is looking something up, write notes in the ticket about what you are doing and why.

---

## Common Call Scenarios

### Customer Calls About a Late Order
- Pull up the order immediately using the order number.
- Check ready time, at-restaurant time, picked-up time, delivered time, and expected travel time.
- Communicate the current status honestly and apologize for any delays.

### Customer Calls About Missing Items
- Confirm the order number on the bag.
- Ask for a photo of the receipt.
- Determine fault (restaurant or courier) before offering resolution.

### Customer Calls Wanting to Speak to a Supervisor
- Do not argue. Create or assign a ticket to a supervisor with all relevant information.
- Let the customer know the supervisor will reach out to them.

### Restaurant Calls with an Issue
- Help them directly if possible (confirm orders, disable menu items, troubleshoot tablet).
- If you cannot resolve it, create a ticket and assign appropriately.

### Courier Calls About Their Shift
- Monday-Friday 8am-4pm: Direct to Driver Relations.
- Outside those hours: Handle the adjustment yourself and find coverage if needed.
