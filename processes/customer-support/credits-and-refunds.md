# Credits and Refunds -- How to Issue

> Source: Dispatch Analyst Guide -- How to credit/refund section
>
> This document covers the technical procedure for issuing credits and refunds. For policies on when to issue them, see `refund-policy.md`.

## Step-by-Step Procedure

### Step 1: Open the Order

- Click the **order ID** or enter it in the search bar to open the order.
- Click **"Order Corrections"** in the top right of the page.

### Step 2: Set Resolution to Refund

- Once in Order Corrections, set the resolution to **"Refund"**.

### Step 3: Choose the Fault Assignment

Select who is financially responsible:

- **Restaurant Owes Valley Eats**: The restaurant will be charged by Valley Eats for the issue. This means when we credit/refund the customer, it does not come out of Valley Eats' pocket. Use this when the restaurant made the mistake.
- **Valley Eats Owes Restaurant**: Used when a driver was behind and we need to credit the restaurant for a remake. This is for when Valley Eats or a courier caused the problem (misdelivery, remake needed). This field does not need to be filled unless the restaurant is remaking food at Valley Eats' cost.

### Step 4: Choose Refund Type

- **Refund Cash**: The entered amount is refunded back to the customer's original payment method. It may take **1-3 business days** for the customer to see the refund, depending on their bank.
- **Refund Credits**: Adds a credit amount to the customer's Valley Eats account. This is **instant** -- if a customer plans to reorder right away, use credits. The customer may need to refresh their app to see the change.

### Step 5: Write Notes

- **Always** write notes explaining why the credit/refund is being issued in the description field.
- Notes should be as detailed as possible and include:
  - What was done
  - The reasoning for it

### Step 6: Assign Driver Error (if applicable)

- If the issue was caused by a driver error, select the type of error from the dropdown box.

### Step 7: Save

- Press **"Save"** and ensure no errors are produced.

## Service Fee Credits (Separate Process)

Service fee credits are issued differently from order refunds:

1. Open the customer's profile (not a specific order).
2. Click the **"No fees"** button in the top right corner.
3. Enter the number of service fee credits to add.
4. To remove credits, enter a negative number.

Service fee credits waive the service fee on future orders and are automatically applied at checkout.

## Sending Proof of Refund to Customers

- Open the customer's transaction history (bottom of customer profile).
- Click the **paper scroll icon** on the far right of the transaction to bring up the receipt.
- Take a **screen snip** (Windows: Shift+Win+S) of the receipt.
- Send the screen snip to the customer. **Do not copy the receipt link** -- always send the screenshot.

## Key Rules

- **Never use the general "credit" button** on the customer profile. Always go into a specific order to issue credits so notes can be attached.
- **Goodwill credits** (extra compensation beyond the issue value, e.g., an extra $5 for inconvenience) must always be issued as credits, never as cash refunds. Confirm with a supervisor or manager before issuing goodwill credits.
- Cash refunds take 1-3 business days. If the customer is reordering immediately, use credits instead.
- If there is a driver error, always select the error type in the dropdown so it is tracked.
- Refund amounts for food quality issues follow a severity scale (see `refund-policy.md` for thresholds). Major credits require supervisor approval.
