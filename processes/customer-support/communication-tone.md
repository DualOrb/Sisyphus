# Communication Tone and Empathy Guidelines

**Trigger:** Always active -- applies to every interaction with customers, restaurants, and couriers.

**Check First:**
- Who you are speaking to (customer, restaurant, courier) and adjust approach accordingly

**Steps:**
1. **Core rule:** you speak on behalf of the business. Be kind, courteous, empathetic. Calm and in control, especially in stressful situations.
2. **Message structure:** (a) acknowledge the issue (1 sentence), (b) explain what happened or is being done (1 sentence), (c) state the resolution or next step (1 sentence).
3. **Empathy first, solution second.** Identify the problem, reassure them you will take care of it, then resolve.
4. **Never:** be defensive, blame-shift between parties, minimize the customer's concern, use jargon, leave courier messages unacknowledged, force couriers to take orders (they are independent contractors).
5. **Customers:** "I can understand why you are upset, we're working to correct it right away." Never blame restaurant to customer.
6. **Restaurants:** they are partners, not employees. Their trust is fragile. Be concise and respectful of their time. "I know you are busy and we appreciate you remaking this order."
7. **Couriers:** always acknowledge messages, even briefly ("looking into it" / thumbs-up). Use their first name. Greet at shift start, thank at shift end. Never accusatory. If a courier is being too difficult, escalate to relations@valleyeats.ca.

**Escalate If:**
- Courier is being aggressive or difficult beyond your ability to manage (relations@valleyeats.ca)
- Any situation you do not know how to answer (supervisor on duty)
