# Ticket Assignment and Reassignment Guide

How to assign and reassign tickets in the Valley Eats ticket tracker.

---

## Assigning Tickets to Yourself

When a new issue comes in, immediately assign it to yourself by:
- Clicking the "owner" dropdown menu and selecting yourself, or
- Clicking "assign to me."

This is critical so that everyone on the team knows the issue is being looked at and being resolved.

---

## Assigning Tickets to Others

Before assigning a ticket to someone else, verify all of the following:

1. **Right person.** Make sure you are assigning the ticket to the correct person or team.
2. **Needs to be assigned.** Consider whether this is something you can handle yourself. For example, a menu change that comes in on a Friday night will not be addressed until the following week if assigned to the restaurants team. If you can do it, take care of it. If not, ask a supervisor.
3. **Include specific information.** Explain clearly why you are passing the ticket along and what outcome you are looking for.
4. **Reset the status to "New."** After assigning to someone else, change the status back to "New" so the new owner can immediately see they have a new ticket requiring attention.

## When to Reassign

There are specific situations where you cannot or should not handle an issue yourself.

### Restaurant is Closed

- Assign to the support agent or supervisor next on shift when the restaurant is open.
- Let the customer know we will get in touch when the restaurant opens again.
- Leave a detailed description of all steps you have taken.

### Unable to Reach Customer

- If it is urgent and you are nearing the end of your shift, assign to the next support person.
- Leave all relevant notes.
- Notify the person switching on to continue trying to reach out.

### Restaurant Menu / Portal / Tablet Issues

- If you cannot resolve it yourself, create a ticket with all relevant information.
- Assign to a supervisor so they can ensure all needed information was captured.
- The supervisor will forward to growth@valleyeats.ca to resolve.

### App / Web Issues

- First, try all steps in the "Technical Troubleshooting" section of the guide.
- If unresolved, create a ticket with all information and assign to a supervisor.
- The supervisor will ensure all information is present and forward to the technical team.
- Always consult with a supervisor first — they may have more experience with these issues.

### Courier Issues

- If you have repeated negative courier interactions (between you and the courier, or between the courier and the customer), create a courier ticket with all information.
- Submit to Driver Relations who will forward to the relations team.

### Customer Wants to Speak with a Supervisor

- This is not always your fault. Some customers are difficult and will try to get more out of an inconvenience.
- Create or assign a ticket to a supervisor with all relevant information.
- Let the customer know the supervisor will reach out to them regarding the issue.

## Escalation Path Summary

```
Dispatcher (You)
  |
  +--> Supervisor (fraud, app/web issues, major credits, customer escalations)
  |      |
  |      +--> growth@valleyeats.ca (menu/portal/tablet issues)
  |      +--> Technical / Dev team (app bugs, broken orders)
  |      +--> Further action as needed
  |
  +--> Driver Relations / relations@valleyeats.ca (courier shifts, courier behavior)
  |      (Monday-Friday, 8am-4pm only)
  |
  +--> Next support agent on shift (restaurant closed, unable to reach customer, end of shift)
```

---

## Key Rules

- Anything you cannot resolve yourself must be notated in a ticket so it can be reviewed and escalated.
- After reassigning, always mark the ticket as "New" so it stands out to the new owner.
- Never leave a ticket unassigned. If you are working on it, it should be assigned to you. If you are passing it, assign it to the next person.
- Staff phone numbers in the call center contacts are confidential. Never give a staff member's number to a customer or restaurant without their permission.
