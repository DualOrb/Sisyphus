# Customer Retention

**Trigger:** Always active -- context for every customer interaction.

**Check First:**
- Customer complaint history (how many issues in last 30 days)
- Is this their first complaint or a pattern?

**Steps:**
1. **Why it matters:** 95% of customers with bad experiences tell someone. In small towns, bad news travels fast (social media, Facebook groups, App Store reviews). Good experiences drive referrals -- our best marketing channel.
2. **Strategy: empathy first, resolution second.** Let customer explain without interruption. Acknowledge frustration as legitimate. Own it (don't deflect to restaurant/courier). Resolve quickly. Follow through.
3. **Strategy: go above and beyond.** Proactively offer credit before customer asks. Follow up after resolution. Be flexible within policy to favor customer experience.
4. **Protect restaurant reputations:** never blame restaurant to customer. Handle resolution ourselves. Communicate with restaurant separately using empathy.
5. **Build courier relationships:** greet at shift start, thank at end, acknowledge messages promptly. Appreciated couriers perform better.
6. **Retention signals:** first complaint = resolve generously. 2nd in 30 days = extra care, proactive credit. 3+ in 30 days = escalate to supervisor (pattern). Customer mentions competitor = decision moment. Threatens to leave = resolve above and beyond. Negative social media = escalate immediately.

**Escalate If:**
- Customer has 3+ issues in last 30 days (systemic problem)
- Negative social media mention
- Customer threatening to leave and resolution exceeds your authority
