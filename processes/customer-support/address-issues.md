# Address Issues

> Source: Dispatch Analyst Guide -- Addresses section, Change in Address, Wrong Address

## Changing a Customer's Delivery Address on an Active Order

### Step-by-Step Procedure

1. Open the order itself.
2. Click **"Modify Order"**.
3. Input the new address.
4. Click all **"Geolocate"** and **"Calculate"** options. This recalculates delivery costs to the new address.
5. Write a reason for the change in the **"Modify Notes"** section.
6. **Confirm that the new address marker is in the correct location on the map.**
7. If the order is already in transit, **call the driver** to confirm they received the updated address so they do not misdeliver.
8. Also notify the dispatcher, as the change may affect subsequent orders planned for that courier.
9. Send an email or call the customer confirming the address has been updated.

### If an Error Occurs During Address Change

1. Open a new dispatch tab/window and try again.
2. Search for the address on Google Maps and ensure it is within the delivery radius for that market. Click the red circle on the map to see the delivery radius outline.
   - **If outside the delivery zone**: Inform the customer their new address is outside the delivery area. Offer the option to meet the courier at the edge of the zone to pick up the order.
   - **If inside the zone**: Continue troubleshooting.
3. Ensure the **town name** is correct. Some areas have multiple valid town names that map differently. For example, the Smiths Falls area includes Montague, Rideau Ferry, Lombardy, Jasper, and Drummond/North Elmsley. Try alternate names and see which pins closest to the actual location.
4. Ask the customer if they are in a **new subdivision**. New subdivisions take time to appear in the mapping system (HERE Maps, a third-party system).
   - If it is a new subdivision: find a nearby address that works and ask the driver to deliver to the actual address. Alert a supervisor -- they may be able to request a map update.
   - Anyone with a free HERE Map Creator account can submit map updates.
5. If nothing works, **escalate to a supervisor**.

### Apple Pay / Google Pay / Android Pay Limitation

- If a customer used Apple Pay, Android Pay, or Google Pay, an error will occur when changing the address because Valley Eats cannot charge the extra delivery fee through these methods.
- **If the customer used Android Pay or Google Pay**: Valley Eats covers the additional delivery cost, because it is less expensive than canceling and paying the restaurant for the food.

### Fixing the Address in the Customer's Account

- After resolving the address on the current order, **also update the address in their account** so the issue does not recur on future orders.
- Ensure the customer is aware of the changes made to make their address work.

## Using the Map System for Addresses

- Use **wego.here.com** to troubleshoot addresses. This is the same mapping system used by Valley Eats delivery services.
- Use the order modal to input addresses until the pin appears at the correct location on the map.
- Confirm with the customer that the pin is in the correct location.

## Address Pin Colors on the Dispatch Map

- **Green pin**: Order is on track, on time, no conflicts.
- **Red pin**: Order is late and needs attention. If 10+ minutes late, a red box also appears around the order.

## No Unit Number on Delivery Address

1. Click on the customer's email address in the order.
2. Check past orders for a unit number on file.
3. Message the customer from the current order asking them to meet the courier at the front door/main entrance.

## Customer Not Responding at Door

1. Message the customer: "Courier is at your location with your order."
2. If still no response, **call** the customer.
3. Instruct the courier to leave the order at the door and take a photo showing the address/door number.
4. When/if the customer contacts Valley Eats, explain what happened.

## Wrong Address (Order Already Delivered to Wrong Location)

1. Call the courier to find out exactly where they left the order. Couriers take a photo when a customer does not come out to receive the order -- the photo is attached to the order (located above the customer's address in the order view).
2. Call the customer and ensure they have checked everywhere, including where the courier left it.
3. If the order is still not found, offer to **re-dispatch** the order (if the customer wants that option). Process the re-dispatch:
   - Only mark "Driver Error" if you are 100% certain it was the driver's fault.
   - Notify the dispatcher on shift.
   - Request a **different driver** for the re-dispatch.
   - Have the new driver **call the customer upon arriving** and **take a photo of the delivery location including the house number**.
   - Check the driver's location on Google Maps to confirm it matches the house location.
4. After re-dispatch, **follow up with the customer** to ensure they received their meal.

## Cross-Market Deliveries

- Orders with a red or blue background on the dispatch/order side indicate cross-market deliveries (e.g., Pembroke to Petawawa, Carleton Place to Almonte).
- Bundle cross-market orders as much as possible to avoid losing couriers to long trips.
- Try to plan a return trip for the courier (e.g., if they go Pembroke to Petawawa, try to give them a Petawawa to Pembroke order on the way back).
- Avoid giving any single courier more than **3 in-bag orders** at one time to maintain food quality.
