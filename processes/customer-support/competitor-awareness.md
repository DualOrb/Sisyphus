# Competitor Awareness and Differentiation

**Trigger:** Always active -- context for interactions where customers compare or consider switching.

**Check First:**
- Is the customer comparing us to a competitor, considering leaving, or complaining about a bad experience?

**Steps:**
1. **Our disadvantages:** smaller marketing budget, weaker promotions, less brand recognition outside home markets (Pembroke/Petawawa).
2. **Our advantages:** local and personal presence, reputation for caring, direct relationships with all three branches (customers, couriers, restaurants), trusted as ambassadors for restaurant brands.
3. **Core differentiator:** customer service is the weapon. We must offer an amazing level of service to beat international competitors. If customers don't believe we deliver better service, they will choose the cheaper option.
4. **When a customer complains:** this is the critical moment -- they are deciding now whether to stay or switch. Turn the complaint into proof that our service is different.
5. **When a customer mentions competitors/promotions:** do not badmouth competitors. Emphasize what we offer they cannot: local team, personal service, genuine commitment.
6. **New market customers:** need extra proof we are worth choosing. Every interaction must demonstrate the quality.
7. **Every ticket/message/resolution is a competitive moment.** The customer is always comparing their experience with what they could get elsewhere.

**Escalate If:**
- Customer explicitly states they are switching to a competitor (high-value retention opportunity)
- Negative social media mention (escalate immediately)
