# Process: Competitor Awareness and ValleyEats Differentiation

## Purpose

This document outlines what makes ValleyEats different from competitors and how to leverage those differences in customer, restaurant, and courier interactions. Understanding our competitive position helps agents reinforce the value of choosing ValleyEats.

## Competitive Landscape

### What Competitors Have That We Don't

- **Larger marketing budgets** — our large, international competitors spend millions of dollars through PR efforts to maintain their positive image.
- **Better promotions** — competitors' large budgets allow them to offer much better promotions for new customers, and they are very aggressive in trying to steal our customers from us.
- **Brand recognition at scale** — as we move further away from the Pembroke/Petawawa area, the less likely potential customers will recognize us as the "local option." There is less automatic loyalty since we are simply new to them.

### What We Have That They Don't

- **Local, personal, small-town presence** — in our home markets (Pembroke/Petawawa area), customers recognize us as the local option and give us automatic loyalty.
- **Reputation for caring** — our competitors have reputations for poor customer service. We can and must do better.
- **Direct relationships** — our Customer Service Specialists are the only department in contact with all three branches of clientele: customers, couriers, and restaurants. This means every interaction is an opportunity to build a personal connection that large competitors cannot replicate.
- **Restaurant trust** — we are ambassadors for our restaurant partners' brands. Restaurants trust us to treat customers with the same respect and care they would if customers were dining in the restaurant. This trust is something large platforms often erode.

## The Core Differentiator

> **Our customer service is the differentiator when customers are deciding between our service or one of our international competitors. We need to offer an amazing level of service to beat the competition.**

Since we operate on a much smaller scale, we need to focus on our level of service to beat them in our markets. If customers don't believe we will deliver on our promise of a better service, they will always gravitate towards the less expensive option.

**A perfect delivery service is the ultimate goal to beat our competitors. Amazing customer service is the only way to retain those customers if we don't get it right the first time.**

## Key Talking Points for Interactions

When interacting with customers (especially those comparing us to competitors or considering leaving):

1. **We are local** — we operate in and care about the communities we serve. We are not a faceless international corporation.
2. **We are personal** — our team knows the restaurants, the couriers, and the area. Every interaction is with a real person who cares about your experience.
3. **We protect restaurant reputations** — reputations are everything in the restaurant industry, and we treat our restaurant partners' brands as carefully as they do.
4. **We go above and beyond** — when something goes wrong, we own it and make it right. Our size means we can be flexible and responsive in ways large competitors cannot.

## How to Leverage in Specific Situations

### Customer Complaining About a Bad Experience
This is the most critical moment. The customer is deciding right now whether to stay with ValleyEats or switch to a competitor. Your response determines the outcome. Turn the complaint into proof that our service is different — acknowledge the problem with empathy, resolve it quickly, and go above and beyond where possible.

### Customer Comparing Us to a Competitor's Promotion
Do not badmouth competitors. Instead, emphasize what we offer that they cannot: a local team that knows the community, personal service, and a genuine commitment to getting it right.

### New Market Customers (Outside Pembroke/Petawawa)
These customers do not have the built-in loyalty of our home market. They need extra proof that we are worth choosing. Every interaction must demonstrate the service quality that justifies choosing us over a cheaper or more familiar alternative.

### Restaurant Partner Concerns
Restaurants that see our service as a burden, inconvenience, or threat to their reputation will leave the app. This wastes all the time and effort the sales team invested in recruiting them. It could also cause a domino effect in tight-knit restaurant communities. Demonstrate that we take their reputation as seriously as they do.

## What This Means for the Agent

Every ticket, every message, every resolution is a competitive moment. The customer is always — consciously or not — comparing their experience with us to what they could get elsewhere. The agent's job is to make the answer obvious: ValleyEats treats them better.
