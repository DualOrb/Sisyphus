# Email Handling Procedures

How to handle email communication at Valley Eats, including labels, response standards, and special email types.

---

## Overview

Email (Gmail) is a significant way we communicate with customers. Log into Google using your provided support email credentials and select Gmail.

---

## Composing and Sending Emails

### Creating a New Email
1. Press the "Compose" button. A small window will pop up in the bottom right corner.
2. Fill in the recipient's email address.
3. Write the subject line. Use the same subject as the ticket (e.g., "Missing Item," "General Inquiry").
4. Write the body of the email. Each topic should have its own line, with a row of whitespace between topics.
5. Fill in your first name in the footer of the email.
6. Hit "Send."

### Forwarding Information to Restaurants

**Critical rule:** To avoid accidentally forwarding customer information to a restaurant, always create a new email and copy only the details and photos needed. Never forward the original customer email directly.

When emailing a restaurant about an order issue:

1. Ensure all customer details are erased — no email addresses, names, or trackable information.
2. Ensure the email is professional.
3. Provide all needed and relevant information.
4. Be as unbiased as possible. Explain what the customer would like as a resolution, if applicable.
5. Let the customer know we have forwarded their concerns to the restaurant and will get back to them as soon as the restaurant responds.
6. Label the sent email as "To be addressed" and "Waiting for Response" so the restaurant's reply does not get lost.

## Email Labels

Labels organize emails within Gmail. They function similarly to ticket statuses.

### Core Labels

| Label | When to Use |
|-------|-------------|
| **Handled by: [your name]** | Apply whenever you respond to an email in any way. This gives a detailed view of who contributed. Required on every email you touch. |
| **Waiting for Response** | You have sent a reply and are waiting for the other party to respond. |
| **Needs to be Addressed** | Nothing has been done about this email yet. |

Set and remove labels as the situation changes, the same way you would update a ticket status.

### Filtering by Label

All labeled emails can be filtered using the tabs on the left-hand side of the Gmail screen. Click a label to see only emails marked with that label.

### Searching for Emails

Type the customer's email address into the "Search Mail" box and press Enter. You can apply additional filters using the available buttons.

## Special Email Types

These emails require extra attention when they arrive.

| Email Subject | Action Required |
|---------------|-----------------|
| **[Contact]** | This is the web version of a ticket. Treat it as a regular ticket. Write notes as a draft in the email. If it is not the contact form, still treat it the same way and resolve the issue, but ask the customer to update their app. You can check their app version by searching for them on the VE dispatch page under customer info. |
| **Charge Not Captured** | Let a supervisor know the order number. They will look into it to confirm what is wrong with the order. |
| **Notify Error** | The recipient of a ping was not notified. Usually means a driver has their notifications turned off. |
| **Broken Order** | Let a supervisor know and confirm further instructions. The restaurant may need to be notified of the cancellation, and the customer as well. |
| **Roger Panic** | Roger (the smart assignment program) has not been able to assign certain orders. Manual confirmation is needed. Let the dispatcher on that market know so they can correct it. |
| **Problem Address** | Look into the customer's profile for notes on the address. Pass them to the dispatcher on shift. If no information is on file, confirm with the customer by phone the directions to their address. Ensure driver instructions are accurate to prevent errors. |
| **Suspicious Customer** | Same as "Problem Customer." Let the dispatchers on shift know about this customer in case an issue comes in. Alert a supervisor if an issue does come in so they can take appropriate action. |
| **Fraudulent Customer** | Alert a supervisor immediately. A marked fraudulent customer cannot place an order. The supervisor will determine if the block is still needed. |
| **Security Alert** | Alert a supervisor to confirm whether this was a legitimate login or not. |

## Email Templates

Pre-made email templates are available in the Appendix of the Dispatch Analyst Guide. Use and reference them as needed. Below are the standard templates and when to use each.

### Late Delivery — Order ETA Update
Use when a customer's order is delayed and you need to communicate the situation.

**Structure:**
- Order number
- Greeting ("Good Evening")
- Apology for the delay
- Explanation of the situation
- Resolution offered
- Closing ("Best Regards" + your first name)

### Late Delivery — Courier Running Behind
Use when higher-than-normal order volumes are causing delays.

**Key point to include:** "We always communicate with the restaurant to ensure your order is as fresh as possible for its arrival."

### Late Delivery — Wrong Turn
Use when the courier is having difficulty finding the customer's address.

**Key point to include:** "Our driver is currently experiencing difficulties finding your address. I assure you, we are getting your order back on track."

### Missing Items — Simple Credit
Use when crediting for a missing item without re-dispatch.

**Key point to include:** "We always ask our drivers to confirm the order with the restaurant, but unfortunately, we are unable to open the bags to ensure all the food is present."

### Missing Items — Re-dispatch
Use when the missing items are being sent out again.

**Key point to include:** "You may still track your re-dispatch using your app."

### Complete Refund
Use for situations requiring a full order refund. This should be a custom message tailored to each situation.

### Restaurant Out of Stock
Use when you were unable to contact the customer about an out-of-stock item and had to make a decision on their behalf. Explain what resolution was applied and why.

### Internet Service Outage
Use during third-party internet outages affecting operations and restaurant communications.

## Email Prioritization

Handle emails in this order:

1. **Immediate:** Broken Order, Roger Panic, Fraudulent Customer, Security Alert — these affect active operations or security.
2. **High:** [Contact] emails (web tickets), Charge Not Captured, Problem Address — these are customer-facing issues waiting for resolution.
3. **Standard:** Notify Error, Suspicious Customer — important but not time-critical.
4. **Routine:** General inquiries, follow-ups, restaurant communication threads.
