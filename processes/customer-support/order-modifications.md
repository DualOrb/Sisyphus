# Order Modifications

> Source: Dispatch Analyst Guide -- Order Modifications, Modifying Items in the Cart, Adding/Removing Items

## Payment Method Restrictions

Order modifications (adding items) are **only possible with certain payment methods**. Check the payment method by scrolling to the bottom of the order and viewing the method in the transaction section.

### Supported Payment Methods for Adding Items

- Visa
- Mastercard
- Visa Debit (provided there are enough funds in the customer's account)
- Credits

### Unsupported Payment Methods for Adding Items

- Apple Pay
- Google Pay (Android Pay)

If the customer used an unsupported payment method, items cannot be added to the order. Removing/refunding items is still possible.

## Adding Items to an Order

### Step-by-Step Procedure

1. Open the order.
2. Select **"Modify Cart"**.
3. Browse the restaurant's menu and click the item to add.
4. Scroll down and make any modifications needed (e.g., side of fries).
5. Double-check all items in the cart.
6. Click **"Order Details"**.
7. On the order details screen, click all **"Calculate"** options to recalculate delivery costs.
8. Click **"Place Order"** to update the order.

### After Adding Items

1. Confirm with the customer that they accept the extra charges for the added item(s).
2. **Call the restaurant** and notify them of the addition. Specify the order number and items being added. If they refresh their tablet, the addition should appear on their end.
3. Email, ping, or call the customer to confirm the addition has been made.
4. Note all actions in the ticket and dispatcher notes.

## Removing Items from an Order

### Step-by-Step Procedure

1. Confirm exactly which items need to be removed.
2. **Call the restaurant** to let them know not to prepare the specified items.
   - If the restaurant says the order/item is already made, try not to remove it -- removing already-prepared items may require Valley Eats to refund the restaurant.
3. In the order, click the **"X"** beside the item to remove it, then click **"Submit"**.
4. On the details screen, click all **"Calculate"** options to recalculate the new total.
5. Click **"Place Order"** to update the order.
6. Write your process in the ticket. Confirm with the customer that the items have been removed and refunded to their payment method.

## Restaurant Does Not Have an Item

When a restaurant calls in to say they cannot fulfill a specific item:

1. Ask the restaurant if there are any **substitutions** available (e.g., no chocolate chip cookies but M&M cookies are available).
2. **Call the customer** to see if they would accept a substitution.
   - If the substitution costs **more** than the original: go into "Modify Order," remove the unavailable item, and add the new item. The customer will be charged the difference.
   - If the substitution costs **less**: calculate the difference, click "Place Order" to adjust pricing.
3. If you cannot reach the customer: write all information in the **"Dispatcher Notes"** on the order and send a ping asking them to call in about an order issue. This way any dispatcher who takes the call has the information needed.
4. Relay the customer's decision back to the restaurant so they know whether to substitute or hold the item.
5. If ready time arrives and the customer has not responded, make a judgment call:
   - A similar item (e.g., a smaller size) is generally acceptable to send.
   - A completely different item (e.g., skittles instead of beef jerky) is not -- refund the item instead.
6. After resolution, notify the customer of what was done and why.

## Key Rules

- Always confirm with the customer before adding items or charging extra.
- Always notify the restaurant of any changes to the order.
- Always document modifications in tickets and dispatcher notes.
- If a payment method is unsupported for modifications, inform the customer of the limitation.
