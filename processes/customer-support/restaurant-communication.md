# Process: Restaurant Communication Guidelines

## Purpose

This document defines how agents communicate with restaurant partners. Restaurants are the lifeblood of the business, and every interaction must reinforce trust. This guide applies to all agents who interact with restaurants: Customer Support, Task Executor, Market Monitor, and Driver Comms.

## Core Principles

**Source:** Dispatch Analyst Guide -- Restaurants, Customer Service Specialist as the Voice of Our Partners.

### 1. Restaurants Are Partners, Not Employees

Restaurants do not work for Valley Eats. Valley Eats provides a service to them. Every interaction must reflect this dynamic:
- They are trusting us with their reputation
- They chose to work with us and can choose to leave
- We are ambassadors for their brand when delivering for them

### 2. Reputation Is Everything

Reputations are everything in the restaurant industry. Restaurant owners are very careful about protecting their reputations. Trusting Valley Eats with their reputation is a significant step for any restaurant to make. Never take this trust lightly.

### 3. Fragile Trust with New Partners

The sales team does not have many opportunities to build relationships and trust in person, so the trust with new restaurant partners is especially fragile. A single negative interaction with dispatch or support can undo months of sales team effort.

### 4. The Domino Effect

If restaurants see the service as a burden, inconvenience, or threat to their reputation, they will leave the app. This:
- Wastes all the time and effort the sales team invested (trips, recruiting, negotiating, menu input)
- Can cause a domino effect in tight-knit restaurant communities where owners talk to each other

### 5. Restaurants Are Stressful Environments

It is important to understand that restaurants are stressful work environments. Taking time to communicate with us unnecessarily can be upsetting to them. Be concise, respectful, and purposeful in every contact.

## Using Empathy to Maintain Trust

**Source:** Dispatch Analyst Guide -- Using Empathy to Maintain Trust (Restaurant Section).

The best way to maintain and rebuild the trust of restaurant partners about order errors or courier issues is to use clear communication to identify and empathize with their issues. The relationship between Valley Eats and restaurant partners is very important, and we want to do everything we can to preserve it.

Trust is broken when we make errors and need to re-dispatch food. Empathy helps maintain and rebuild that trust.

### Example Empathy Phrases for Restaurant Interactions

Use these phrases (or natural variations) when speaking with restaurant partners:

**When asking a restaurant to remake an order:**
> "I know that you are busy and we appreciate you remaking this order for us."

**When an error has occurred:**
> "I'm so sorry this has happened, what can we do to make sure it won't happen again?"

**When working through a problem together:**
> "Thanks for working with us here. We're doing everything we can to make sure the customer is being taken care of."

**When calling about an unconfirmed order:**
> "Hi, this is [name] from Valley Eats. Sorry to bother you -- I just wanted to let you know there's an order waiting to be confirmed on your tablet when you get a chance."

**When a restaurant is frustrated with a courier issue:**
> "I completely understand your frustration, and I apologize for the inconvenience. We're addressing this on our end right away."

**When asking about a halt or hours change:**
> "No problem at all -- we'll get that updated for you right away. Is there anything else you need from us?"

**When troubleshooting tablet issues:**
> "I know this is interrupting your service and I want to get this resolved as quickly as possible for you."

## Communication Channels

### Tablet Messenger

The messenger on the restaurant tablet is the communication gateway between dispatch and restaurant partners. Use it to:
- Notify the restaurant when a driver is delayed for pickup
- Request that an order be delayed or sped up
- Remind the restaurant about an order that needs to be confirmed

**However:** We need to call restaurants directly if an issue arises, as we know they may not always be checking the tablet. The messenger is best for non-urgent notifications; the phone is for anything time-sensitive.

### Phone Calls

Call the restaurant directly when:
- An order is approaching pickup time and has not been confirmed
- There is a time-sensitive issue that needs immediate resolution
- Tablet troubleshooting requires real-time guidance
- A substitution needs to be arranged on an active order
- Any urgent operational issue

**Phone call best practices:**
1. Be brief -- restaurants are busy
2. Identify yourself immediately: "Hi, this is [name] from Valley Eats"
3. State the purpose clearly and quickly
4. If they sound rushed, offer to call back or keep it as short as possible
5. Always thank them at the end of the call

### When the Restaurant Calls Us

The restaurant full-time support number is **1-855-610-3287**. Restaurants are instructed to call this number when:
- A substitution to an order has to be made
- A product or menu item has run out
- Any driver-related issues
- Any time-sensitive issues that need to be resolved
- Tablet and/or internet issues
- General support
- If a customer calls the restaurant about an order, the restaurant should direct them to call us at the same number or use the in-app help button

## Tone and Language Guidelines

### Do

- Be warm, professional, and concise
- Acknowledge that they are busy before making your request
- Thank them for their time and cooperation
- Apologize sincerely when Valley Eats is at fault
- Offer solutions, not just problems
- Frame requests as collaborative: "Can we work together to..." rather than demands
- Let them know what you are doing to fix the issue on your end

### Do Not

- Sound impatient or accusatory, even if the restaurant missed an order
- Blame the restaurant to the customer (frame delays as "preparation is taking a bit longer")
- Call unnecessarily -- every interruption costs them time during service
- Make demands -- they are partners, not subordinates
- Ping the tablet excessively (see cooldown rules in `restaurant-unconfirmed-orders.md`)
- Use overly formal or scripted language that sounds robotic -- be human and genuine

### Handling Difficult Situations

**If a restaurant is upset or frustrated:**
1. Listen first -- let them express their concern fully before responding
2. Acknowledge the problem: "I understand, and I'm sorry this happened"
3. Take ownership where appropriate: "That's on us, and we're going to fix it"
4. Provide a clear path forward: explain exactly what you will do to resolve the issue
5. Follow through -- if you promise to do something, do it and update them

**If a restaurant threatens to leave the platform:**
1. Do NOT argue or make promises you cannot keep
2. Empathize: "I understand your frustration and I don't blame you for feeling that way"
3. Escalate to supervisor immediately -- this is a relationship management issue
4. Document the conversation in a ticket with full detail

**If a restaurant is uncooperative (won't confirm orders, won't respond):**
1. Try multiple contact methods (messenger, phone)
2. Be patient -- they may be in the middle of a rush
3. If they remain unresponsive, consider halting to prevent more unconfirmed orders from stacking up
4. Document and escalate -- persistent unresponsiveness may indicate a relationship issue for the Restaurant Relations Coordinator

## Communication When Redirecting Customers

If a customer calls the restaurant directly about a Valley Eats order, the restaurant should redirect them to:
- **Phone:** 1-855-610-3287
- **In-app:** Help button in the Valley Eats customer app

If a restaurant reports that customers are calling them directly about delivery issues, thank them for letting you know and reassure them that you will handle it.

## Audit Note

While communication tone is not formally audited like system actions, agents should be aware that:
- Phone calls may be reviewed for quality
- Tablet messenger history is logged and reviewable
- Tickets capture the full interaction history
- Restaurant satisfaction directly impacts business retention

Maintain the same professional, empathetic standard in every interaction, whether it is being reviewed or not.
