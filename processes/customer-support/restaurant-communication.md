# Restaurant Communication Guidelines

**Trigger:** Any interaction with a restaurant partner.

**Check First:**
- Is this time-sensitive (call) or informational (tablet messenger)?
- Restaurant's current operational state

**Steps:**
1. **Core principles:** restaurants are partners, not employees. Their trust is fragile, especially for new partners. A single negative interaction can undo months of sales effort. Treat their reputation as carefully as they do.
2. **Use tablet messenger for:** non-urgent notifications (driver delayed, order reminders). **Use phone calls for:** anything time-sensitive (unconfirmed orders approaching pickup, substitutions, troubleshooting).
3. **Phone best practices:** be brief, identify yourself immediately ("Hi, this is [name] from Valley Eats"), state purpose clearly, thank them at the end.
4. **Tone:** warm, professional, concise. Acknowledge they are busy. Apologize sincerely when Valley Eats is at fault. Frame requests as collaborative. Never sound impatient, accusatory, or blame restaurant to customers.
5. **When asking for a remake:** "I know you are busy and we appreciate you remaking this order for us."
6. **If restaurant is frustrated:** listen first, acknowledge, take ownership, provide clear resolution path, follow through.
7. **If restaurant threatens to leave:** do NOT argue or make promises. Empathize, escalate to supervisor immediately, document fully.
8. **Customer redirect:** restaurant support number is 1-855-610-3287. If customers call restaurant about a delivery issue, restaurant should direct them to this number or in-app help.

**Escalate If:**
- Restaurant threatens to leave the platform
- Restaurant is persistently unresponsive (may need halting and relationship management)
- Restaurant is upset and situation is beyond your ability to resolve
