# Courier Communication Guidelines

**Trigger:** Always active -- applies to every courier interaction.

**Check First:**
- Does the courier have active orders? Reference the specific order by orderIdKey.

**Steps:**
1. **Fundamental rules:** couriers are independent contractors. Always respectful, professional. Always acknowledge messages (even "looking into it" or thumbs-up). Never accusatory. Never argue. Use first name. Keep messages under 160 chars when possible. Never blame driver for system issues. Never promise specific ETAs without confirmed data.
2. **Message vs call:** message for routine updates, acknowledgments, non-urgent. Call when rerouting an en-route courier (they may not check phone while driving), when time-sensitive, or when you are too busy to type.
3. **Channels:** in-app messenger (standard), SMS push (urgent, direct to phone), SNS push (less urgent, app only), phone call (reroutes, time-sensitive).
4. **Shift structure:** start = greet all couriers, open communication. During = respond to every message, fix issues promptly. End = thank couriers for their work.
5. **Response limits:** max 2 messages before waiting for response. Min 3 min between unsolicited messages. Follow-up if no response: 5 min wait, max 3 attempts.
6. **Working with courier concerns:** if a courier says orders won't work for them (timing, routing), try to move those orders to another courier. Less frustration for everyone.
7. **If courier is too difficult:** do not engage further. Email or ticket relations@valleyeats.ca.
8. **Repeated behavior issues:** make a ticket for relations@valleyeats.ca.

**Escalate If:**
- Driver is threatening, abusive, or unsafe to drive
- Physical safety concern
- 3 follow-ups with no response
- Order cancellation/reassignment unclear
- Financial impact >$50
- Policy dispute with no clear answer
- Any situation where you are unsure
