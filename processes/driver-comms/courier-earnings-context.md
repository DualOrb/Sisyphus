# Courier Earnings Context

Use this document as context when handling pay-related questions or complaints from couriers.

## Base Pay Structure

| Component | Rate |
|-----------|------|
| Base rate (first 5 km) | $4.50 |
| Per-km rate after 5 km (standard range) | $0.61/km |
| Per-km rate after 5 km (extended delivery range) | $0.80/km |

- Distance is calculated based on the **restaurant-to-customer distance**, not the courier's current location on the map.
- The system calculates the delivery fee automatically.

## Tips

- **Couriers receive 100% of the tip** on delivery orders.
- For takeout orders, the tip goes to the restaurant — not the courier.
- Dispatch does not look at the tip on an order before assigning it to a driver. Tips are not a factor in order assignment.

## Payout Schedule

- Couriers are paid for the current week's earnings (Saturday to Friday) on the following Friday.
- Earnings are deposited directly into the courier's bank account.
- If a courier has questions or concerns about receiving their payout, direct them to **relations@valleyeats.ca**.

## Earnings Visibility in the Driver App

Couriers can view their earnings in the app's Earnings View:
- Broken down by daily, weekly, monthly, and yearly
- Shows number of deliveries, trip earnings, and tips

## Top-Ups (Distance Adjustments)

When drivers deliver orders a certain distance from one town to another, they may be eligible for a top-up. Top-ups are added via the order's delivery adjustments.

**Important: Top-ups should not be added to an order until it is completed/delivered.**

### Carleton Place driver with an Almonte-to-Almonte order
- **$4.00 top-up** (plus $0.50 gas top-up = **$4.50 total**) when a Carleton Place driver picks up in Almonte and delivers to an Almonte address.
- If the driver gets 3 or more Almonte orders while in Almonte, they no longer qualify for the top-up.
- NOT eligible if the order goes from Carleton Place to Almonte.
- NOT eligible if the order goes from Almonte back to Carleton Place.

### Embrun driver with a Casselman-to-Casselman order
- **$4.00 top-up** (plus $0.50 gas top-up = **$4.50 total**) when an Embrun driver picks up in Casselman and delivers to a Casselman address.
- If the driver gets 3 or more Casselman orders while in Casselman, they no longer qualify for the top-up.
- NOT eligible if the order goes from Embrun to Casselman.
- NOT eligible if the order goes from Casselman back to Embrun.

### Carleton Place or Perth driver with a Law & Orders Innisville order
- **$4.00 top-up** (plus $0.50 gas top-up = **$4.50 total**) when a courier picks up from Law & Orders Innisville and delivers to a Perth address.
- NOT eligible if the courier picks up from Law & Orders Innisville and delivers to Carleton Place.

## Customer-Side Delivery Fee (for reference)

The delivery fee charged to the customer is:
- $4.00 base fee
- $0.61/km in base range
- $0.80/km in extended range
- This varies by market.

Note: The customer delivery fee and the courier pay are separate calculations.

## Courier Perks (Points System)

Couriers earn points through various activities:
- Submitting availability before every Tuesday at midnight
- Picking up shifts
- Delivering orders with no misdeliveries

Points are **deducted** for:
- Misdelivering an order
- Dropping shifts
- Missing items or incorrect orders (only penalized if the receipt was shown on the outside of the bag)

Couriers can redeem points in the driver shop for:
- Valley Eats credits
- Gift cards
- Valley Eats merchandise

Couriers can also connect their courier account to their customer account to transfer accumulated points into credits on their customer account.

## Handling Pay-Related Courier Messages

### Courier doesn't understand the fee calculation
Explain the structure: "$4.50 for the first 5 km, then $0.61/km after that, plus 100% of the customer's tip. In-town orders pay less, out-of-town orders pay more."

### Courier thinks there was a pay mistake
Direct them to email **relations@valleyeats.ca** with the order number. The system calculates fees automatically — dispatch cannot manually override the base calculation.

### Courier refuses an order because the pay is too low
You cannot force a courier to take an order. Ask them politely to deliver it. If they refuse, move the order to another courier and write a ticket for relations@valleyeats.ca.

### Courier asks about payout timing or missing payout
Direct them to **relations@valleyeats.ca** — they handle all payout concerns.

### Courier complains about only getting no-tip orders
Acknowledge the frustration, but explain that dispatch does not see or consider tips when assigning orders.
