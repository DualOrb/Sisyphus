# Courier Best Practices

As a customer service specialist/dispatcher, it is your job to ensure couriers are following delivery protocol. Use this document as context when evaluating driver behavior or giving feedback.

## Order Status Marking Rules

Couriers must mark order statuses accurately and only when the status is actually true:

1. **"En Route"** — Only mark when actually on the way to the restaurant. Restaurants receive a notification when the courier is en-route and will attempt to prepare the order accordingly. Marking en-route prematurely causes restaurants to rush or time food preparation incorrectly.

2. **"At Restaurant"** — Only mark when actually at the restaurant. This allows dispatch to keep accurate track of the courier's whereabouts and order status.

3. **"In Bag"** — Mark once the order is in the courier's possession. If the courier has multiple orders, leave them as "In Bag" and only put one order at a time into "In Transit."

4. **"In Transit"** — Only set when actually on the way to the customer. Do not mark until you are physically leaving with that specific order.

5. **"Delivered"** — Mark once the order is delivered to the customer.

## Pickup and Delivery Protocol

- **Arrive at the restaurant a few minutes before pick-up time.** The more the courier stays ahead of the game, the more orders they can potentially do.
- **Use the map to plan your route** — look at order pick-up and delivery locations.
- **Always have the delivery bag** when picking up orders and when delivering to the customer.
- **Confirm the order number** (shown at the top of the order) when picking up at the restaurant.
- **Confirm the contents of the order** before taking it from the restaurant.
- **Double-check order numbers** when picking up and dropping off orders.
- **Do not blindly trust Google Maps.** Double-check the address.
- **Use the customer's name** (if displayed in the order view) to add a personal touch when delivering.

## Efficiency Best Practices

- **Load up as many orders as possible without being late.** More orders in the bag means warmer food.
- **Use logic when mapping out pickups and drop-offs.** Plan the most efficient route.
- **Look at the map in the app** to see order pick-up and delivery locations and plan accordingly.

## Food Handling

- **Don't mix hot and cold food bags.**
- **Drinks:** If possible, take drinks out of the tray and put them in cup holders. Otherwise, place them on the floor of the vehicle and try to brace them so they don't spill.
- **Pizza trick:** Most pizzas fit in the bags. If an extra-large pizza won't fit regularly, place the bag on its side with the flap down and open, slide the pizza in, and press on the bag so you can zip it up.

## Phone and Connectivity

- **Always have your phone on you and have it charged up.** Carry a charger in the car if able.
- **Notifications must be enabled** to receive order notifications in the app.
- **Must be logged in** to receive orders.
- **Turn off WiFi at restaurants.** Some restaurants (like McDonald's) offer free WiFi that requires login. When a courier's phone connects, it stops the data function and the app goes blank.

## Courier Is the Face of the Company

Couriers are uniquely identified by the green Valley Eats delivery bag. They represent the company to every customer they interact with. Behavior and presentation matter.

## Delivery Bags

- Couriers must use the green Valley Eats bag for all deliveries.
- A second bag can only be handed out if cleared with Courier Relations.
- If a driver asks for a second bag, reach out to Courier Relations before providing one.

## Common Courier Support Scenarios

### No Unit Number on Delivery Address
1. Click on the customer's email address in the order.
2. Check past orders for a unit number.
3. Message the customer from the current order asking them to meet the courier at the front door/main entrance.

### Customer Not Responding to Door
1. Message the customer: "Courier is at your location with your order."
2. If still no response, call the customer.
3. Courier leaves the order at the door and takes a photo with the address/door number.
4. When/if the customer contacts Valley Eats, explain the situation.

### Courier Will Be Late
- **Late to restaurant:** Message the restaurant — "Courier is delayed, should be there in ______"
- **Late to customer:** Message the customer — "Our apologies for the delay, your order will be on its way shortly."

### Courier Marked Order Incorrectly
- Change the order status to the correct status in dispatch.

### Courier Picked Up Wrong Order
- Change the courier on the order they picked up.
- Change the courier on the order they should have picked up.

### Orders Not Displaying in Courier App
1. Make sure the courier has orders assigned to them.
2. Check that the courier has a working data connection and that WiFi is turned off (restaurant WiFi can break the app).
3. Have the courier shut down the app and reload. Send the courier a test message.

### Courier Overloaded
1. Find the most efficient route for orders and communicate it to the courier.
2. If unable to complete any orders, find an available courier and reassign.

## Evaluating Courier Behavior

When assessing whether a courier is following best practices, check for:

- [ ] Are they marking statuses accurately and at the right time?
- [ ] Are they arriving at restaurants on or before pickup time?
- [ ] Are they using their delivery bag?
- [ ] Are they confirming order numbers and contents at pickup?
- [ ] Are they double-checking delivery addresses?
- [ ] Are they responsive to dispatch messages?
- [ ] Are they handling food properly (hot/cold separation, drink security)?

### Addressing Issues
- For one-off or minor issues: address respectfully and directly with the courier if you feel comfortable.
- For repeated patterns of poor behavior: make a ticket for relations@valleyeats.ca.
- If you are not comfortable addressing the issue yourself: ask a supervisor or make a ticket for relations@valleyeats.ca.

## Reminder: Couriers Are Independent Contractors

- They choose their own hours.
- They work solely off commission for deliveries.
- They cannot be "forced" to take orders or work shifts.
- Positive relationships lead to better cooperation. Being kind to couriers means they will be kind back and more willing to help in tough situations.
- Dispatch is always there to help the couriers — that is part of the role.
